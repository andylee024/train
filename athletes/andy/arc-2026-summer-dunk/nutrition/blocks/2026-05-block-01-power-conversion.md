# Block 01 — POWER CONVERSION + UPPER BUILD (Nutrition)

## Header
- Block: B1 of 3 — Power Conversion + Upper Build
- Weeks: W1–W6 (May 3 – Jun 13, 2026)
- Pairs with: `training/blocks/2026-05-block-01-power-conversion.md`
- Kcal phase: −300 deficit

## Purpose
Establish the cut while reactive base is being built. Drop bw without compromising the small-but-important strength gains in B1's bench/pull-up volume base. Build the eating habit before complicating it with cooking.

## Goals
- Bw at end of block: ~188 lb (down from 192)
- Kcal target: ~2400 kcal/day
- Protein target: 190g/day (acceptable range 170-200g)
- Adherence target: 80% on-plan
- Calibration: validate the 2400 kcal hypothesis at Wk 2 (per arc.md Testing/Calibration)

## Strategy
B1 is the eating-habit-building block. The cut starts immediately because the deficit is mild (−300) and protein is high — strength is protected. But cooking volume is intentionally minimal: this block is about getting the daily eating right (Layer 1 supplements + 1 batch dinner + leftovers), not about kitchen complexity. Per Andy's failure mode (falls off when complexity creeps in), B1 establishes the *floor* of effort. Variety unlocks in B2.

## Weekly Structure
**Cooking pattern: 1 batch/week (Sunday only)**

The week runs on one Sunday cook session + leftovers + Layer 1 supplements + rotisserie chicken pickup mid-week for variety. Zero weeknight cooking on the standing menu. Total kitchen time: ~45 min/week (Sunday batch + brief assembly).

Practical cadence:
- **Sunday evening:** batch one D-tier dinner (D1 chicken thighs is the workhorse) — make 4-5 portions
- **Mon-Wed:** leftovers + Layer 1 + rotisserie chicken assembly
- **Thu-Sat:** rotisserie chicken bowls (L1, L6) or one fresh ground beef pan if motivated (D3)
- **Layer 1 always-on:** Fairlife AM, Greek yogurt PM, biltong as snack (carries ~75g/day protein)

## Standing Recipes (most-likely picks this block)

The orchestrator will pick from these 5 meals in B1, given the cooking pattern:

- **D1** sheet-pan chicken thighs + sweet potato + frozen broccoli — L effort, heavy carb (Sunday batch workhorse, 4-5 portions)
- **D3** ground beef pan + rice + frozen broccoli — M effort, heavy carb (optional Wed batch in B2; in B1, only if motivated)
- **L1** rotisserie chicken bowl + rice + greens — Q effort, heavy carb (lunch backup, fresh rotisserie pickup)
- **L6** chicken + sweet potato bowl — Q effort, balanced (lunch from leftovers)
- **L8** leftover dinner — Q effort (the default lunch when D1 is fresh)

## Heavy/Light Day Fueling

- **Heavy days (Thu Speed-Strength A, Sat Jump Lab):** carb-heavy dinner (D1 or D3 leftover), B5 oatmeal+whey breakfast, post-training banana + 1 scoop whey within 30 min of last set
- **Light days (Wed rest, Sun light squat):** lighter meals OK — L3 cottage cheese for lunch on rest day, smaller dinner portion
- **Post-training (training days only):** 1 banana + 1 scoop whey, non-negotiable on Thu and Sat

## Calibration Moments

- **Wk 2 (≈ 2026-05-23):** first 2-week test per arc.md Testing/Calibration. Compare actual bw loss rate to expected (0.5-0.8 lb/wk). Adjust kcal target if outside range.
- **Wk 5:** mid-block check — squat top set holding ≥360 lb? If dropping >5%, halve deficit per Constraints.
- **Wk 6 (end of B1):** target bw 188 lb; assess transition to B2 (cooking pattern unlocks 2nd batch).

# Block 03 — PEAK + REALIZE + TEST (Nutrition)

## Header
- Block: B3 of 3 — Peak + Realize + Test
- Weeks: W13–W18 (Jul 26 – Sep 5, 2026)
- Pairs with: `training/blocks/2026-07-block-03-peak-realize-test.md`
- Kcal phase: **Maintenance — NO CUT** (per VJ §9)

## Purpose
Peak. The lighter, stronger body built in B1+B2 realizes the dunk against a 10' rim. Nutrition's job in B3 is to **stop cutting and fuel performance** — eating supports force production, not bw drop. The cut was the runway; B3 is the flight.

## Goals
- Bw at end of block: **184–186 lb (HELD, not dropping)** — defend the cut, don't push it further
- Kcal target: ~2700 kcal/day (recalibrated maintenance)
- Protein floor: ≥190g/day (still non-negotiable)
- Adherence target: 80%+ on-plan (sat dunk attempts every week → fueling matters)
- Nutrition test: bench 1RM 260 lb + pull-up +80 × 8 + the dunk itself

## Strategy
B3 is the inverse of B1+B2 — instead of optimizing for bw drop, optimize for force production. Per VJ §9, cutting during peak compromises power output. So: maintenance kcal, full carb refeed quality, and the cooking pattern expands further to support the eating volume increase. **The 2700 kcal target is +300 over B2's 2400 — that's an extra meal or larger dinner portions every day.** Variety becomes important because eating volume goes up — Andy needs to actually want to eat at this level.

## Weekly Structure
**Cooking pattern: 3-4 cook sessions/week**

The kitchen is fully open. Sunday batch + Wednesday batch + 1-2 weeknight cooks + occasional treat (D4 ribeye, D5 burger night). Total kitchen time: ~90-120 min/week.

Practical cadence:
- **Sunday evening:** main batch (D1, D6, or D13 whole roasted chicken — the latter is a "force multiplier" feeding 4-5 meals)
- **Mon-Wed:** Sun batch leftovers + Layer 1
- **Wednesday evening:** second batch (D3 ground beef or D17 beef and broccoli)
- **One weeknight cook:** D2 sheet-pan salmon, D7 chicken stir-fry, D14 bulgogi, or D18 larb (block-relevant pick based on training load)
- **One treat/weekend:** D4 ribeye, D5 burger night, or D11 salmon poke
- **Total kitchen time:** ~90-120 min/week (still bounded — peak isn't the moment to add complexity)

## Heavy/Light Day Fueling

- **Heavy days (Thu Speed-Strength A, Sat Jump Day + Dunk Attempts EVERY week):** heavy-carb dinner, B5 oatmeal+whey breakfast, banana + scoop whey within 30 min post-training, EXTRA carbs Fri dinner before Sat dunk attempts
- **Sat = TEST DAY (W18):** carb-load Fri dinner (rice + sweet potato), B5 oatmeal+whey breakfast Sat AM, banana 30 min before warm-up, electrolytes during session, post-attempt full meal + extra protein for recovery
- **Light days (Wed rest, Sun light squat):** still ~2700 kcal — don't undereat just because it's a light training day. Use larger Layer 1 portions or extra snack if appetite is low.
- **Post-training (training days only):** 1 banana + 1 scoop whey, non-negotiable

## Calibration Moments

- **Wk 13 (block start):** transition from cut to maintenance. Bw should hold (no further drop). If bw keeps dropping after switching to 2700, raise to 2800.
- **Wk 15:** strength performance check. If lifts haven't responded to maintenance kcal (squat top set still depressed from cut), increase kcal +100-200.
- **Wk 18 (TEST WEEK):** maximize fueling for test days (bench 1RM, pull-up max-reps, dunk). Carb-load 24h before each test.

## Transition (from B2 → B3)

- **Kcal target jumps +300/day** (from 2400 cut → 2700 maintenance). This is the biggest single change in the arc.
- **Cooking pattern unlocks fully:** 3-4 sessions/week + treat nights become regular.
- **Bw stops dropping** (this is the whole point — defend the lighter body, don't push it further).
- **Strength prioritized over body comp.** If a tradeoff appears (e.g., social meal that would push kcal over), choose the meal — adherence to maintenance > adherence to a ceiling that doesn't exist.
- **Saturday treats become regular** (D4 ribeye, D5 burger night) — they're maintenance-friendly and serve the morale side of peak.

# Train — Product Requirements Document

**Status:** v0 prototype shipped; AI synthesis + execution loop pending
**Owner:** Andy Lee
**Updated:** 2026-05-26

---

## 1. Vision

> **Train is the AI coaching team that lets any athlete design and execute a serious training plan as easily as they shop for a coach.**

You browse the world's best coaches, pick the ones whose philosophies resonate, and Train synthesizes their programming into one structured plan you can actually run. As you train, Train delivers your daily session, captures your logs, and shows you whether you're getting better — across strength, body comp, and movement.

It is **not** a workout-logging app. It is **not** a generic AI fitness chatbot. It is the *system that connects intent to execution to evidence* across an entire training arc.

---

## 2. Who it's for

### V0 user (now)
**Andy Lee** — solo builder + sole athlete. Training a 2026 summer dunk arc. Highly motivated. Allergic to operational overhead. The product must work for him before anyone else.

### V1 user (next)
Athletes who currently follow YouTube coaches but can't get a personalized plan from them. They know who they trust (Nippard, Cbum, Fraser, P3, etc.) but don't know how to combine those influences into a coherent program.

### Not the target
- Couch-to-5k beginners (we assume some training literacy)
- Competitive athletes with paid coaches already (we're cheaper substitute)
- Generic "track my workouts" users (Hevy / Strong already serves them)

---

## 3. The job-to-be-done

> *When I'm working an 18-week training arc toward a real goal, I want my plan to drive my day automatically — so all my mental energy goes into training, not into managing the plan.*

The friction the product removes:
- Picking what to do (replaced by coach marketplace + AI synthesis)
- Remembering what's planned today (replaced by daily SMS prescription)
- Logging what was done (replaced by text-message log + AI parser)
- Knowing if I'm improving (replaced by metrics dashboard)
- Knowing when to adjust (replaced by weekly review brief)

What it does NOT replace: the actual coaching judgment of *how* to train, *what philosophy* to follow, *whether the plan is right for you*. The athlete keeps that. Train just removes the operational drag.

## 5. Product principles

### Design
1. **No composite indices.** Every metric on every dashboard traces to a concrete event.
2. **Discrete > continuous.** Bars and tables beat smoothed curves.
3. **Declines surface with equal weight to growth.** Stalls and regressions are visible, not buried.
4. **Athlete-agnostic backward-looking core.** Pillars (/strength, /nutrition, /movement) survive arcs. Goals/plans live in the arc.
5. **Density without crowding.** Tight rhythm; generous letter-spacing on labels; tabular numerics everywhere.
6. **Fixed visualization vocabulary.** Numbers, sparkbars, line charts, diverging bars, heatmaps, tables, tags. No new chart types without a system-level decision.
7. **No coach voice in the dashboard surface.** Voice lives in the REVIEW module (SMS/email digests).

### Plan creation
1. **Shop for people, not categories.** "Jeff Nippard" not "Hypertrophy methodology." Identity transfer is the unit of decision.
2. **The marketplace IS the preference signal.** Picking Cam Davidson tells us more than any survey would. Don't ask users to describe themselves; let them shop.
3. **AI synthesizes, athlete chooses.** AI weaves the picks into a plan; athlete sees the work and confirms before committing.
4. **Two-step intake at most.** Goals + days/wk + optional constraints. Anything else asked later or inferred.

### Architecture
1. **Storage boundaries are sacred** (see `docs/product/database-schema.md`):
   - Planned work → markdown in arc bundle
   - Executed work → Supabase
   - Athlete-facing view → derived `.xlsx`
2. **The xlsx is the contract.** Generated by AI synthesis. Consumed by the execution agent. Renderable in the dashboard.
3. **Decisions are working hypotheses.** Saved to `docs/product/decisions.md` with date + rationale, so we can revisit.

- Payments

- Coach-side tools (managing other athletes)
- Video form analysis
- Voice memo logging
- Plan modification via SMS ("change tomorrow")
- Block retro automation (manual for V1)
- Movement pillar quantitative data (ROM tests not yet wired)

## 7. Success criteria

By end of V1, Andy should be able to:

1. **Wake up Monday** → phone has today's session waiting (SMS, by 6:30 AM PT)
2. **Train** → text-log the workout in <30 seconds
3. **See logs on the dashboard same-day** → no batch lag
4. **Repeat 6 days/week** without manual intervention
5. **Sunday morning** → 2-minute scan of the dashboard tells me what's working and what's stalling
6. **Start a new arc** (mid-September) by shopping coaches + AI synthesis, without writing a plan by hand

Anything that makes Andy open a terminal, write SQL, or hand-edit markdown as part of the normal weekly cycle = V1 failure.

## 9. Risks + open questions

### Risks
1. **AI synthesis quality** — if the AI produces a bad plan, the whole product fails on first use. Mitigation: keep human-in-the-loop on plan generation; allow regeneration; show your work in preview.
2. **SMS parser reliability** — if logs are misparsed, athletes lose trust fast. Mitigation: every inbound SMS persisted raw; failed parses surfaced for review; confirmation reply makes errors visible immediately.
3. **Cost** — Twilio + Modal + LLM tokens at $5-$10/month/athlete. At 100 users that's $500-$1000/month before revenue. Mitigation: keep cheap with text-only; defer voice/video.
4. **Coach licensing** — the marketplace uses real coach names and likenesses. Mitigation: contact coaches as we scale; for V1 (Andy-only) it's fair-use commentary.

### Open questions (deferred to specific specs)
- Custom-style ingestion model (user adds a coach not in library)
- Mobile breakpoint shape
- "Anything to know?" notes routing (synthesis vs ongoing context)
- Block retro automation: triggered by what, delivered how
- Subscription model: per-athlete? per-coach? freemium?

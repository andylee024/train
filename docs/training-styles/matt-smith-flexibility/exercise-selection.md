## Schema

This catalog uses an adapted schema because the strength-focused master library doesn't have the right columns for flexibility work.

Each entry has:

- **method** — one of the 10 categories below
- **skill** — which of the 8 flexibility skills it serves (or "general" for cross-cutting methods)
- **spectrum** — position on assisted ↔ resisted continuum: `heavily_assisted`, `assisted`, `neutral`, `resisted`, `heavily_resisted`
- **role** — `isolation` (opens range for the next stretch) or `compound` (the skill itself) or `technique` (alignment drill) or `prep` (capsule/nerve/soft-tissue work)
- **typical_loading** — sets × reps × hold time (with cited source where given)
- **equipment** — array of needed items
- **notes** — when to use, conditional rules, contraindications

## Method 1 — PIR (Post-Isometric Relaxation / Contract-Relax)

Spectrum: assisted. Role: isolation. Mechanism: brief antagonist or agonist contraction → muscle relaxes deeper than passive baseline for a few seconds → settle into the new depth → repeat.

**Variant 1a — Classic Contract-Relax (CR):** contract the muscle being stretched.
**Variant 1b — Antagonist Contract-Relax (ACR):** contract the *opposite* muscle (reciprocal inhibition).

| Setting | Typical loading | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| General | 3–5 sets × 5–8 reps × ~5-s contract + ~5-s relax-into-depth | When the limiter is passive ceiling (small range, no end-range strength gap) |
| End-range work | 3 sets × 5 reps with controlled deeper settling on each rep | When pushing established range deeper |

## Method 2 — Isometric Stretching

Spectrum: middle / resisted. Role: isolation or compound (some skills *are* isometrics — bridge, sometimes split).

| Setting | Typical loading | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Long single hold | 1–2 sets × 20–30 s at near-max depth | When the goal is end-range tissue tolerance (less preferred than method 3 — see notes) |
| Skill-is-an-isometric (bridge) | 3 sets × 10–30 s | For skills like the bridge that *are* the held position |

**Note:** Matt explicitly prefers **isometric-holds-for-reps (Method 3)** over single long holds. Use this method when the skill itself dictates a long hold; otherwise default to Method 3.

---

## Method 3 — Isometric Holds for Reps *(Matt's most-programmed method)*

Spectrum: middle / resisted. Role: compound (most common use), can be isolation.

Multiple short holds with brief rests between, instead of one long hold. Each rep can go deeper than the previous because the inter-rep relaxation permits incremental settling.

| Setting | Typical loading | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | 3–5 sets × 5–10 reps × **3-s hold** with ~3–5 s between reps | **Default** for the compound skill stretch at the end of a session, and for most resisted isolation work |
| End-range strengthening | 3 sets × 5 reps × 3-s hold at progressively deeper positions across the set | When building strength-in-range (Sunniva-archetype athlete) |

**Why it works:** "Compare an isometric horse stance to a horse squat. Your adductors will get a better stimulus from the reps because of the short rests in between." Each rep is a fresh attempt at depth.

## Method 4 — Active Lifts (± Eccentric Overload)

Spectrum: resisted. Role: isolation (build strength-in-range) or technique-aligned.

Contract the antagonist to *pull yourself* into the position. No passive assist.

**Standard active lift:** lift the limb actively into range, hold briefly, lower with control.
**Eccentric overload variant:** assist into a deeper range than active strength alone allows (e.g., lift bent-knee, then straighten the leg in the air), then lower slowly under the new heavier eccentric load.

| Setting | Typical loading | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Standard active lift | 3 sets × 6–10 reps × 1–2 s end-range hold | When passive range exceeds active range — close the gap |
| Eccentric overload | 3 sets × 4–6 reps, eccentric phase 3–5 s | When the top of the active range plateaus; build strength above current active ceiling |

## Method 5 — Dynamic Stretching

Spectrum: resisted, velocity-loaded. Role: skill-transfer; engages velocity-sensitive muscle spindles.

| Setting | Typical loading | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | 2–3 sets × 8–15 controlled high-speed reps through the target range | When the goal flexibility must transfer to fast movement (kicks, sprints, throws, acrobatics) |

> NOTE: Source distinguishes dynamic stretching from ballistic. Dynamic uses controlled speed; ballistic uses momentum to bounce.

---

## Method 6 — Ballistic / Rapid Contract-Relax (RCR)

Spectrum: assisted, velocity-loaded. Role: isolation; goes deeper than passive holds permit.

**Classical ballistic:** use momentum to bounce/pulse deeper. Matt learned this from Shaolin monks but moved away from it.
**Rapid Contract-Relax (RCR) — Matt's preferred form:** self-generate the pull deeper using muscle contraction, then release. Athlete controls depth; no momentum-based overshoot.

| Setting | Typical loading | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| RCR | 2–3 sets × 10–20 micro-pulses per set | When passive ceiling is the limiter and PIR has plateaued; deeper micro-stretches than passive hold permits |

**Default to RCR over classical ballistic** unless there's a specific reason for momentum-based work.

## Method 7 — Joint Capsule Releases

Spectrum: position-dependent (not a stretch in the classical sense). Role: prep.

Specific positions that bias load onto the *capsule* rather than the muscle. Used when the athlete feels "stuck" despite muscle relaxation — the capsule is the limiter.

| Setting | Typical loading | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | 2 sets × 30–60 s in the capsular-biased position | When other methods plateau and the restriction feels "deep" / "bony" / "joint-level" |

## Method 8 — Nerve Flosses, Glides, and Tensioners

Spectrum: n/a (nerve, not muscle). Role: prep.

The sciatic nerve only physically stretches ~8–10% beyond resting length. Nerve mobility is mostly about the nerve *sliding* through its sheath. If compressed, entrapment can produce pain, electric sensations, or simply cap range without obvious sensation.

**Flosses:** move proximal and distal joints in opposing directions (lengthening one end while shortening the other) to glide the nerve.
**Glides:** synchronized full-length glide.
**Tensioners:** load both ends simultaneously (use sparingly; can aggravate).

| Setting | Typical loading | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Sciatic floss (toe-touch limiter) | 2 sets × 10 slow reps | When straight-leg-raise + dorsiflexion reproduces symptoms or caps range disproportionate to hamstring length |
| Brachial plexus floss (overhead limiter) | 2 sets × 10 slow reps | When overhead range collapses with neck position changes |
| Femoral floss (front-split limiter) | 2 sets × 10 slow reps | When prone hip extension caps despite hip-flexor work |

## Method 9 — Soft Tissue Work

Spectrum: n/a. Role: prep.

Targeted release of chronically tight muscle, scar tissue, or trigger points. **Through-the-stretch positioning** (place the ball/roller while the muscle is on stretch), not random foam-rolling.

| Setting | Typical loading | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | 1–2 min on the targeted area, positioned in-stretch | When assessment localizes a chronic restriction or palpable trigger point; not as warm-up |

## Method 10 — Technique Drills

Spectrum: n/a. Role: technique.

Not stretching — teaching the body to express existing range with proper alignment.

| Skill | Drill | Cue |
|---|---|---|
| Side split | Shallow-width side split with hip-tilt rehearsal | Tilt hips forward; OR externally rotate legs (point feet up) |
| Bridge | Wall walks (back to wall, hands down) | Lift up first (full elbow + knee extension), then arch over |
| Front split | Tall-kneeling split, back foot tucked | Hips square forward; resist back-hip external rotation |
| Pancake / pike | Hip-hinge first, spine roundness allowed at depth | Hinge from hips, not from waist |

## Substitution map

| If unavailable / contraindicated | Substitute | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Resistance band (for muscle-isolation assessment + PIR) | Partner manual positioning | Same isolation, equipment-free |
| Ankle weights (for resisted active lifts) | Cable column, partner manual resistance, or BW alone | Maintains resisted-spectrum loading |
| Floor-based bridge | Wall walk or elevated-hand bridge (hands on bench) | Reduces shoulder demand while training spine/hip extension |
| Ballistic stretching | RCR | Same depth-overshoot effect with depth-control safety |
| Single 30-s isometric hold | Isometric holds for reps (Method 3) | Better stimulus per session per Matt's stated preference |
| Foam rolling as "warm-up" | Through-the-stretch soft tissue work positioned per assessment | Targeted vs random; addresses an identified limiter |

---

## Disallowed in this style

| Practice | Reason for exclusion |
|---|---|
| Full-body stretching routines (one stretch per joint, no skill alignment) | Defeats stretch stacking; produces 1 unit of gain per stretch instead of the 1+1=3 effect |
| Stretching the day after heavy strength/sport as "active recovery" | Calcium-calpain fatigue pathway means brain bandwidth is saturated; adaptation stalls |
| Compound-skill stretch first in session, isolations after | Defeats stretch stacking; the compound has no opened range to express |
| Forcing depth before fixing technique (e.g., side split without hip tilt) | Bone-on-bone impingement; pain without progress |
| Long static holds when isometric-holds-for-reps is available | Lower-quality stimulus per Matt's coaching experience |
| Generic "more is better" volume bias | Brain adaptations require recovery; high-volume crowds them out |

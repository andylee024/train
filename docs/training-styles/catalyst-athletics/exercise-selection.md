# Catalyst Athletics — Exercise Selection

> **~80 exercises** filtered for Catalyst Olympic weightlifting style + Catalyst-specific variants.
> Source: `training-styles/exercises.md` master library + Catalyst-specific exercises pulled from Everett 2016 (catalog at pp. 955–1126).
>
> Annotated with Catalyst-style programming notes: typical loading, role (primary/accessory/remediator), and when to use.

## Schema

Same as the master library, with two additions:

- **role** — `competition` (the lifts themselves), `primary_strength` (squat, pull, etc.), `variant` (block, hang, segment versions), `accessory` (trunk, mobility, supplemental), `remediator` (technique fixes), `assistance` (overhead, pulling, etc.)
- **catalyst_loading** — typical % range and rep scheme per Everett's prescriptions

## Competition Lifts (the foundation — every program contains these)

| Exercise | Movement | Intensity | Role | Catalyst loading | Style notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Snatch** | olympic | max | competition | 70-100%, 1-3 reps × 3-6 sets | The primary metric. Always present. Use 1-3 reps; 3+ reps degrade speed and technique. |
| **Clean & Jerk** | olympic | max | competition | 70-100%, (1+1) to (2+1) × 3-6 sets | The other primary metric. Often programmed as complex (e.g., 2 cleans + 1 jerk). |
| **Clean** (no jerk) | olympic | max | competition | 70-100%, 1-3 reps × 3-6 sets | Used when isolating the clean for technique or strength focus. |
| **Jerk** (from rack) | olympic | high | competition | 70-100%, 1-3 reps × 3-6 sets | Trained from rack when isolating the jerk; preserves CNS for clean work. |

## Primary Strength (foundational; ≥2× per week in preparation mesocycles)

### Squat — quads / glutes

| Exercise | Movement | Intensity | Role | Catalyst loading | Style notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Back Squat** | squat | high | primary_strength | 70-100%, 1-6 reps × 3-5 sets | The strength foundation. High-bar position preferred (transfers better to Olympic positions than low-bar). |
| **Front Squat** | squat | high | primary_strength | 70-100%, 1-5 reps × 3-5 sets | Catches mirror the clean rack position. Train both. |
| **Pause Squat** (3-5 sec) | squat | high | variant | 70-90%, 2-5 reps × 3-4 sets | Builds bottom-position strength + reverses momentum loss. Useful when clean recovery is weak. |
| **Pendulum Squat / Belt Squat** | squat | medium | accessory | "Mod-Hvy" by feel, 8-10 reps × 3 | Knee-dominant accessory; spares the spine on heavy days. (Per `exercises.md` — may need to add) |
| **Bulgarian Split Squat** | lunge | high | accessory | "BW + DB 25-50 lb" or as tolerated, 8/leg × 3 | Single-leg balance + strength. Doesn't replace bilateral. |

### Pull — hamstrings / glutes / back

| Exercise | Movement | Intensity | Role | Catalyst loading | Style notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Snatch Pull** | olympic | high | primary_strength | 85-110% of best snatch, 2-5 reps × 3-5 | Trains the pull mechanics at supra-snatch loads. |
| **Clean Pull** | olympic | high | primary_strength | 85-110% of best clean, 2-5 reps × 3-5 | Same for the clean. |
| **Snatch Deadlift** | hinge | high | primary_strength | 85-110%, 3-5 reps × 3-5 | Pulling-strength base; slower, postural emphasis. |
| **Clean Deadlift** | hinge | high | primary_strength | 85-110%, 3-5 reps × 3-5 | Same for clean pulling. |
| **RDL** | hinge | high | primary_strength | "wave 5,5,4,3 @ moderate-heavy" | Posterior chain accessory; Andy's program uses 4×5,5,4,3 progression. |
| **Stiff-Legged Deadlift** | hinge | high | primary_strength | Same as RDL | Same role. |
| **Good Morning** | hinge | medium | accessory | 50-70% squat, 5-8 reps × 3 | Spinal-erector + hamstring; lower-load alternative to RDL. |
| **Glute-Ham Raise** | hinge | medium | accessory | BW or BW+5-10, 8 reps × 3 | Hamstring isolation; very Olympic-relevant. |

## Competition-Lift Variants (rotate across mesocycles)

### Hang variants (start from above the floor — typically knee or mid-thigh)

| Exercise | Catalyst loading | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Hang Power Snatch** (knee / mid-thigh / high) | 70-85%, 1-3 × 3-5 | Lighter day; trains 2nd pull + power receiving |
| **Hang Snatch** (knee / mid-thigh / high) | 70-90%, 1-3 × 3-5 | Lighter than from-floor snatch; emphasizes the upper portion of the pull |
| **Hang Power Clean** (knee / mid-thigh / high) | 70-85%, 1-3 × 3-5 | Same for clean |
| **Hang Clean** (knee / mid-thigh / high) | 70-90%, 1-3 × 3-5 | Same |

### Block variants (bar starts on blocks at specified height)

| Exercise | Catalyst loading | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Block Power Snatch** / **Block Snatch** (knee / below knee) | 70-90%, 1-3 × 3-5 | Trains acceleration from dead stop (no pre-stretch); reduces leg/back fatigue vs. from-floor |
| **Block Power Clean** / **Block Clean** | 70-90%, 1-3 × 3-5 | Same |

### Segment lifts (pause at intermediate position)

| Exercise | Catalyst loading | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Segment Snatch** (pause at knee or mid-thigh) | 60-80%, 1-3 × 3-5 | Technique remediation; trains posture at the pause point |
| **Segment Clean** (pause at knee or mid-thigh) | 60-80%, 1-3 × 3-5 | Same |
| **Halting Snatch / Clean Deadlift** (pause at knee or mid-thigh) | 80-100%, 2-5 × 3-4 | Postural strength; common fix for "leading with hips" |
| **Segment Snatch / Clean Pull** | 80-110%, 2-5 × 3-4 | Pulling-mechanics fix |

### Complexes (multiple lifts performed without resetting)

| Exercise | Catalyst loading | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Snatch Pull + Snatch** | 70-85%, (1+1) or (2+1) × 3-5 | Reinforces correct pull before the full lift |
| **Hang Snatch + Snatch** | 65-80%, (1+1) × 3-5 | Position-specific priming |
| **Halting Snatch DL + Snatch** | 65-80%, (1+1) × 3-5 | Fixes hip/shoulder timing |
| **2-Position / 3-Position Snatch** (e.g., floor + knee + mid-thigh) | 65-80%, (3 reps total) × 3-5 | Multiple positions consecutively; reinforces multiple parts of the pull |
| **Clean + Front Squat** | 70-85%, (1+1) × 3-5 | Builds recovery strength alongside the catch |
| **Clean + Jerk** | 70-95%, (1+1) × 3-5 | The competition pair as a complex |

## Technique Remediators (used selectively per athlete's needs)

| Exercise | Movement | Role | Style notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tall Snatch / Tall Clean** | olympic | remediator | Pure 3rd-pull practice; starts from full extension, focuses on elbow drive + foot placement. Light loads only. |
| **Muscle Snatch / Muscle Clean** | olympic | remediator | No re-bend of knees; pure overhead/turn-over strength. 50-70%, sets of 3-5. |
| **Drop Snatch** | olympic | remediator | Drop under from standing; trains speed + precision under bar without leg drive. 60-100% × 1-5. |
| **Snatch Balance** | olympic | remediator | Dip + push under + receive; trains overhead position + speed. 60-110% × 1-5. |
| **Dip Snatch / Dip Clean** | olympic | remediator | Quarter-squat dip + drive; trains leg drive + bar proximity. 60-85% × 1-3. |
| **Snatch / Clean from Power Position** | olympic | remediator | Static start at mid-thigh; isolates 2nd pull. 60-80% × 1-3. |
| **Everett Snatch Pull** | olympic | remediator | Push-back drill; teaches lat engagement for bar proximity. Light loads, 3-5 reps. |
| **Slow-Pull Snatch / Clean** | olympic | remediator | Deliberately slow 1st pull; fixes "rushing off the floor" + leading with hips. 60-75%. |
| **Floating Snatch / Clean Deadlift** | hinge | remediator | No floor reset between reps; builds postural strength. 80-110%, 2-6 reps × 3-4. |
| **Snatch / Clean with No Jump** | olympic | remediator | Feet stay planted; remediation for forward-jump tendency. 60-80%. |
| **Clean-Grip Snatch** | olympic | remediator | Narrow grip = harder turnover; mobility-dependent. 60-80%. |
| **Lift-Off** | hinge | remediator | First-pull-only deadlift (bar to mid-thigh); fixes start-position weaknesses. |

## Plyometrics / Jump Training (per Catalyst Ch. Jump Training pp. 663-674)

Used in preparation mesocycles primarily; reduced in competition mesos; **eliminated within 10 days of competition**.

| Exercise | Movement | Intensity | Role | Catalyst loading | Style notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Box Jump (countermovement)** | plyometric | medium | accessory | 4-5 sets × 3-5 reps, 1-3 days/wk | Standard variant; arms held at chest to isolate leg drive. |
| **Box Jump (non-CM, static start)** | plyometric | medium | accessory | Same | Pure explosive concentric; no stretch reflex. |
| **Squat Jump (full depth)** | plyometric | high | accessory | Same | Trains explosive squat — useful for clean recovery. |
| **Back Squat Jump** | plyometric | medium | accessory | ~20% squat 1RM, 3-5 reps × 3-4 sets | Loaded jump for general elasticity. |
| **Quarter Squat Jump** | plyometric | high | accessory | 30-60% squat 1RM, 3-5 reps × 3-4 sets | Elasticity + explosiveness for 2nd pull. |
| **Jumping Squat** (concentric-only from blocks/rack) | plyometric | high | accessory | Heavy, 3-5 reps × 3-4 | Pure concentric; eliminates need to absorb landing. |
| **Depth Jump (18-28 in)** | plyometric | max | accessory | 2-4 sets × 5-10 reps, ≤3 days/wk for advanced | The most demanding jump variant. Introduce only after foundation; reduce/eliminate near competition. |
| **Standing Long Jump (2-leg / 1-leg)** | plyometric | medium | accessory | 10-40 reps total/wk | GPP staple; key for Level 0-1. |
| **Standing Vertical Jump** | plyometric | medium | accessory | 5-20 reps total/wk | GPP + CMJ-test capacity baseline. |
| **Running Long Jump (5-7 step approach)** | plyometric | high | accessory | 3-10 reps total/wk | GPP advanced variant. |

## Substitution map (Train-specific)

| If unavailable / contraindicated | Substitute | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Snatch from floor | Block snatch (knee or below knee) | Reduces lumbar load; preserves 2nd-pull training |
| Clean from floor | Block clean | Same |
| Full snatch (overhead mobility limit) | Power snatch | Avoids deep overhead squat |
| Full clean (front-rack mobility limit) | Power clean | Avoids deep front squat |
| **Front rack catch (wrist injury — Andy)** | Hang variants only + power versions | Skip the loaded front-rack catch entirely |
| Behind-neck jerk / press | Front-rack push press | Removes shoulder impingement angle |
| Heavy back squat (back issue) | Front squat or pause squat at moderate load | Reduces lumbar shear |
| Heavy deadlift (back issue) | Snatch / clean pull from blocks | Reduces moment arm from floor |
| Jerk dip (knee issue) | Push press to overhead | Removes the dip-and-recover demand |
| Olympic lifts entirely (joint injury) | Jumps + squats + presses + sled / heavy throws | Preserves power adaptation pathway through alternative high-velocity work |
| Bumper plates unavailable | Move to Olympic-pulling-only programming + sandbag throws/cleans + box jumps | The catches require bumpers — without them, restrict to pulls and accessory power work |

## Disallowed in this style

| Exercise | Reason for exclusion |
|---|---|
| **High-rep competition lifts (>3 reps at near-maximal)** | Neurological fatigue degrades speed; defeats the speed-strength training adaptation |
| **Slow / paused concentric on Olympic lifts** | Olympic lifts must be trained with maximal concentric speed — pause-eccentric is OK as a variant; slow-concentric is contraindicated |
| **Behind-the-neck jerk / press for Andy** | Per profile — R-shoulder contraindication |
| **Front-rack hold or catch for Andy** | Per profile — L-wrist (De Quervain's) contraindication; hang variants only |
| **Excessive variation per cycle** | Catalyst's view: rotate variants between mesocycles, not within — frequent change prevents tracking adaptation |
| **Pure powerlifting low-bar squat as primary** | Low-bar position trains different hip/knee mechanics; high-bar squats transfer better to Olympic positions |
| **Heavy bodybuilding-style isolation as primary** | Fine as accessory; competing for recovery if elevated to primary |
| **Endurance / metcon-style conditioning** | Direct SAID conflict with speed-strength adaptation |
| **Cardio (long-distance running, cycling > 30 min)** | Same as above; suppresses Olympic-specific recovery |

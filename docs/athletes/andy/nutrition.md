# Nutrition — Andy Lee

Last updated: 2026-04-30

The system that keeps protein on target without cooking, by combining a whey supplement, a pipeline of pre-cooked natural proteins, and the work cafeteria for vegetables.

---

## The picture

- **Bodyweight:** ~192 lb, cutting to 182–185 lb (Goal #5)
- **Protein target:** **190g/day** (1g per lb bodyweight; non-negotiable during cut to protect strength)
- **Cooking:** doesn't cook routinely; prefers natural food over processed
- **Work pattern:** lunch is at the office, vegetables (and likely some carbs) are available there
- **The bottleneck:** protein. Vegetables and carbs are solved by the office. Bringing or having dense, natural protein within reach is what fails.

The strategy: **supplement + pre-cooked natural proteins + a small fallback menu.** No meal subscription, no cooking sessions.

---

## Three-layer system

### Layer 1 — Daily protein floor (the supplement)

Reliable, repeatable, requires no decisions.

- **AM Fairlife** — 42g
- **AM Greek yogurt** — 20g (optional, brings AM to ~62g)
- **Whey shake (1–2 scoops)** — 25–50g, mid-morning or post-workout

**Recommended whey:** **Naked Whey** — single ingredient (grass-fed whey, US dairies), no flavoring, no sweeteners. Most "natural" option. ~$80 / 5 lb (76 servings) = ~$30–40/mo at 1–2 scoops/day. Alt: **Transparent Labs Grass-Fed Whey Isolate** if you want flavored.

This layer alone delivers **~80–100g/day** with zero prep effort.

### Layer 2 — Pre-cooked natural protein pipeline

Bought, not cooked. The Sunday session is a **restock**, not a cook session. All items are heat-and-eat or eat-cold.

| Item | Protein | How to use |
|---|---|---|
| Just Bare grilled chicken bites (Costco, frozen) | 22g per 3oz | Microwave 3 min at work, eat with office salad bar |
| Costco rotisserie chicken | 30g per 4oz, ~70g per half | Eat cold or warm; dinner default |
| Smoked salmon (Kirkland) | 16g per 3oz | Eat cold on greens; lunch or snack |
| Pre-cooked shrimp (Costco frozen ring) | 18g per 3oz | Salad topper, thaw cold |
| Carne asada / pre-marinated steak | 25g per 4oz | Pan-sear 5 min if motivated; dinner |
| Hard-boiled eggs (Costco 6-pack) | 6g each | Snack, gym bag, desk drawer |
| Wild Planet tuna pouch | 17–22g | Desk drawer emergency, shelf-stable |

### Layer 3 — Always-stocked snacks

The friction-zero protein hits between meals.

| Item | Protein | Where it lives |
|---|---|---|
| Greek yogurt (Fage 0% / Two Good) | 20g/cup | Home fridge |
| Cottage cheese (Good Culture 4%) | 25g/cup | Home fridge |
| Biltong (real biltong, not jerky) | ~30g/oz | Gym bag, desk, car |
| Fairlife Core Power | 42g | Home fridge — backup AM or PM shake |

## Fallback menu (when the protein pipeline runs dry)

The fridge being bare is the failure mode this layer covers. All double-protein, all in normal LA orbit.

| Rank | Spot | Default order | Protein |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Chipotle** | Double-chicken or double-steak bowl, brown rice, beans, fajita veg, salsa | 64–72g |
| 2 | **Sweetgreen** | Harvest Bowl or Crispy Rice + double chicken | 40–50g |
| 3 | **Cava** | Greens-and-grains bowl, double chicken or lamb | 40–50g |
| 4 | **Costco food court** | Rotisserie chicken or chicken caesar (also pick up a whole rotisserie for the fridge) | 50–70g |
| 5 | **Travel / no time** | Biltong + Fairlife + 2 boiled eggs from the bag | 70–80g |

**Cap:** ≤1 fallback day per week. If you're hitting fallbacks 3x/week, the Costco restock didn't happen — fix the cadence, not the menu.

## Failure modes and recovery

Pre-decided responses for the patterns that have killed nutrition consistency before:

- **No Sunday restock happened.** → Lean on Layer 1 (whey + Fairlife + yogurt = ~80g locked) and the fallback menu. Mid-week mini-run (Trader Joe's 15 min) if needed. Don't skip restock two Sundays in a row.
- **Traveling / out of town.** → Whey scoops + Fairlife + biltong in the bag. One restaurant meal/day = double-protein order. Don't aim for 190g; aim for ≥160g.
- **Social dinner.** → Order protein-forward (steak, chicken, fish — not pasta). Pre-load the day with whey + lunch protein so dinner is bonus.
- **Fell off for >3 days.** → Don't rebuild from a perfect plan. Reset = next Sunday's Costco restock. Single-decision recovery.

## What this doc is NOT

- Not a meal plan with recipes — too brittle, decays fast.
- Not a calorie-tracking app — if tracking is needed, that's a separate decision.
- Not a stand-in for a registered dietitian — consult one if cut stalls or strength drops noticeably.

This is the **operating system**: a daily supplement floor + a pre-cooked protein pipeline + a small fallback menu. Read it Sunday morning, don't think the rest of the week.

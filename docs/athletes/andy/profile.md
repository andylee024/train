## History & Patterns

### What's Worked
- **Train Heroic:** Seeing last PRs and trying to beat them drove stronger gains. Numbers to chase = non-negotiable.
- **Simple programs with 1 focus per day:** When complexity dropped, consistency went up. Fixed exercise menu generates momentum.
- **Tracking metrics and doing intentional workouts:** Progress comes from tracking + pushing for adaptation, not "gut feel."

### What Hasn't Worked
- **Complex & long programs:** James' initial program (upper + lower same day, high volume, new exercises) caused failure. Too much time learning movements, not enough lifting. Frustration spiral → quit.
- **No tracking tool:** Without easy tracking, guessing at the gym → cognitive load → unproductive sessions.
- **Scattered tools:** Separate apps for workout logging, nutrition, plan management. Losing history when tools break (Nanobot going down). The scatteredness itself kills consistency.
- **Going by "gut feel":** Not tracking metrics and just going on feel at the gym doesn't produce adaptation.
- **Neglecting nutrition:** Good training without nutrition support = incomplete results.

### Known Pitfalls (Self-Identified)
- ❌ Trying to "fit in" goals that aren't main-path (e.g., MMA, basketball skills as dedicated sessions)
- ❌ Not tracking metrics and going based on "gut feel" at the gym
- ❌ Not maintaining good habits for nutrition
- ❌ Being afraid to challenge a coach's plan or going too light
- ❌ Falling off for 2-3 weeks when things get complicated or tools break

### What Success Requires (Self-Identified)
- 🏋️ Consistently dedicate time to the gym
- 🧘 Consistently prioritize daily mobility & flexibility
- 📊 Track metrics and do intentional workouts that push for adaptation
- 🛌 Ensure recovery is on-point (nutrition & sleep)
- 📗 Adapt workouts and habits when things change

# Andy's Menu

Cross-arc menu library. Costco-sourced, athletic-grade, **whole-foods first** — single-ingredient or near-single-ingredient items, minimal processing, no industrial seed oils or ultra-processed packaged foods. The orchestrator picks weekly from this menu based on the active arc's targets, the upcoming week's calendar, and the rotation caps below. **Andy never evaluates the whole menu — he reacts to the weekly pick.**

## Whole-foods principles (the bias behind every row)

- **Single-ingredient defaults.** A meat is a meat, a vegetable is a vegetable. No marinades, no seasoning packets, no breading.
- **Cook fats:** olive oil, butter, ghee, sesame oil. No canola, soybean, vegetable, or "blends."
- **Bread = sourdough only.** Wild-fermented, no commercial brioche, no enriched buns.
- **Dairy = full-fat, minimal-ingredient.** Fage Greek yogurt, Good Culture cottage cheese, real cheese blocks. No string cheese, no "snack packs."
- **Cured/processed meats limited.** Biltong (air-dried, no nitrates) and uncured bacon are the only regulars. No deli turkey, no jerky sticks with sugar/preservatives.
- **No bagged salad kits with industrial dressings.** Romaine, greens, and toppings yes; the dressing comes from olive oil + lemon + salt or homemade.
- **Frozen veg is fine** — just blanched and frozen, no additives. Frozen fries with seed-oil coating are not.
- **Supplements are honest about being supplements.** Fairlife and whey carry the Layer 1 baseline; they're not "food" — they're a protein guarantee.
- **Asian sauces built from single ingredients.** Soy sauce (or tamari), fish sauce (just anchovy + salt), sesame oil, fresh garlic, fresh ginger, chili, lime, honey, rice vinegar. No bottled teriyaki, no curry roux blocks, no commercial marinades, no MSG-laden mixes. Kimchi must be traditionally fermented (cabbage + chili + garlic + ginger + fish sauce), not the sweet supermarket kind.

**Macro framing:** Layer 1 (Fairlife/Greek yogurt/whey) carries ~75g protein/day baseline before any meals. The meals below carry the rest. Macros are estimates — close enough for orchestration, not gram-counting.

**Column legend:**
- **Effort** — Q (Quick, ≤5 min) / M (Medium, 10–20 min) / L (Long, 25+ min)
- **Fit** — Light (≤30g C) / Balanced (30–50g C) / Heavy carb (50g+ C, for heavy training days) / Treat (occasional)
- **Cap** — max times per week the orchestrator will pick this meal (variety guard)

## Rotation rules (for the orchestrator)

1. **Respect caps.** If D1 is capped at 2x/wk, never pick it 3 times.
2. **Balance fueling fit to training week.** Heavy carb meals on Thu (heaviest power day) and Sat (jump day). Light meals on rest days (Wed) and lower-intensity sessions (Sun).
3. **Honor Layer 1 baseline.** Don't double-stack Fairlife if breakfast already includes it.
4. **Default to leftovers.** L8 = "leftover dinner" — orchestrator should size dinner portions 1.5x when L8 is on the next day's lunch slot.
5. **D13 (whole chicken) is a force multiplier.** When the orchestrator picks D13, also size for 2 lunch portions of leftover chicken in the following days (auto-fills L1, L5, L6, L7 substrate).
6. **Treats stay rare.** D4, D5, D12 cap at 1x/wk combined; never two in the same week.
7. **Recipe of the Week** (athlete-chosen, separate from this menu) substitutes for one dinner slot when active.

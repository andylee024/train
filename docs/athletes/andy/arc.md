# Arc Plan — Dunk by End of Summer 2026

## The Goal

Dunk a volleyball or women's basketball on a regulation hoop by the end of August 2026. That's ~18 weeks from today (April 28, 2026).

## Honest Assessment

Andy can grab rim. He squats 370 lb. He has 1.5 years of serious training. He's close — but "close" has been the story for months. The gap isn't strength. The gap is:

1. **Power-to-weight ratio.** At 192 lb and ~15% BF, there's 10+ lb of non-functional mass. Cutting to 183 lb while maintaining strength directly improves vertical.
2. **Rate of force development.** Strength exists but isn't converting to explosive power fast enough. Need dedicated power/plyometric work, not just more squats.
3. **Approach mechanics.** The dunk isn't a standing jump — it's a running approach with specific plant, penultimate step, and arm swing timing. This is a skill that needs practice.
4. **Consistency.** The pattern of falling off for 2-3 weeks at a time is the single biggest threat. The plan must be simple enough to survive bad weeks.

## Priority Stack

When time, energy, or recovery gets tight, this is the triage order:

| Priority | Domain | Rule |
|----------|--------|------|
| 1 | Vertical / Power | Never skip jump day or power work. This is the goal. |
| 2 | Strength (lower body) | Squat and pull maintain the base. Can reduce volume but never drop. |
| 3 | Cut (nutrition) | Stay in deficit. Can slow the rate but never reverse to surplus. |
| 4 | Olympic Lifting | Supports power development. Reduce frequency before dropping. |
| 5 | Upper Body Strength | Maintain only. First thing to cut volume on if recovery is tight. |
| 6 | Flexibility | Daily 10-15 min is non-negotiable but intensity can scale down. |

**What gets dropped first:** Upper body accessories, basketball pickup games, any "extra" work that isn't in the plan.

**What never gets dropped:** Jump training, squat/pull, the cut, flexibility minimums.

## The Theory

The dunk requires roughly: 34-36 inch running vertical (depending on standing reach and arm length). Andy can grab rim, which suggests he's in the 28-32 inch range currently. The gap is 4-6 inches.

Those inches come from three sources:

1. **Losing weight while keeping strength** (2-3 inches). Every 5 lb lost at the same force output adds ~1 inch of vertical. Going from 192 → 183 = ~2 inches.
2. **Converting strength to power** (1-2 inches). Plyometrics, depth jumps, approach work. Andy has the strength base — the nervous system needs to express it faster.
3. **Technical improvement** (1-2 inches). Approach mechanics, penultimate step timing, arm swing coordination. Free inches from technique.

Combined: 4-7 potential inches. That's the gap closed.

## Phase Sequence (5 Phases, 18 Weeks)

### Phase 1 — Reset + Base (Weeks 1-3)
*"Get back on the horse and establish the rhythm."*

**Why this phase exists:** Andy fell off for 3 weeks. The worst thing to do is jump back into heavy loading. This phase rebuilds the habit, re-establishes baseline loads, and starts the cut.

**Training focus:**
- Moderate intensity (RPE 6-7), full exercise menu introduced
- Olympic lifts at 65-70% for technique refresh
- Jump primers only (no max-effort jumping yet — rebuild landing quality first)
- Flexibility work re-established as daily habit

**Nutrition focus:**
- Start controlled cut: ~500 cal deficit
- Protein floor: 200g/day
- Establish meal cadence (prep routine, go-to meals)

**Checkpoints (end of Week 3):**
- ✅ Completed all 5 sessions/week for 3 consecutive weeks
- ✅ Back squat at ≥85% of previous working weight
- ✅ BW trending down (target: 189-190 lb)
- ✅ Zero pain increase in shoulder or wrist

**If checkpoint fails:** Extend Phase 1 by 1-2 weeks. Do not advance with inconsistency — it compounds.

---

### Phase 2 — Strength + Power Build (Weeks 4-8)
*"Build the engine. Squat heavy, jump often, lift fast."*

**Why this phase exists:** This is the primary adaptation phase. Strength goes up, power training begins in earnest, the cut continues. Olympic lifting supports explosive hip extension.

**Training focus:**
- Squat intensity climbs: 75% → 82% across 5 weeks
- Power cleans 3x/week at 72-78% (speed and technique, not grinding)
- Jump training 2x/week: one max-effort day (approach jumps, dunk attempts), one submaximal power day (depth jumps, box jumps, barbell jumps)
- Upper body on maintenance volume (2 sessions/week, moderate intensity)
- Flexibility: long sessions 2x/week, short sessions 3x/week

**Nutrition focus:**
- Cut continues: ~500 cal deficit
- Adjust if strength drops more than 5% — slow the cut, don't abandon it

**Checkpoints (end of Week 8):**
- ✅ Back squat ≥ 365 lb at RPE 8 (maintaining near-peak while cutting)
- ✅ Power clean at 78% feeling fast and crisp
- ✅ BW: 185-187 lb
- ✅ Approach touch improving (measure weekly — track in inches above rim)
- ✅ Dunk attempts show clear progress (closer, higher, more control)

**If checkpoint fails:**
- Strength dropping > 5%? Slow the cut to 250 cal deficit.
- Not hitting jump sessions? Restructure week to prioritize Day 1 = Jump, not Day 5.
- Shoulder/wrist worse? Mobilize specialist agent — propose exercise swaps.

---

### Phase 3 — Power Conversion (Weeks 9-13)
*"Turn strength into speed. Everything gets faster."*

**Why this phase exists:** By now, strength base is solid and bodyweight is dropping into target range. The emphasis shifts from building force to expressing it faster. Volume drops, intensity and speed go up.

**Training focus:**
- Squat volume drops (3x5 → 3x3), intensity holds or increases slightly
- Olympic lifts shift to heavier singles and doubles (80-85%)
- Jump training 3x/week: max-effort approach work becomes primary. Volume of dunk attempts increases.
- Contrast training introduced: heavy squat → box jump, heavy clean pull → depth jump
- Plyometric volume peaks in this phase
- Upper body: 1-2 sessions/week, true maintenance

**Nutrition focus:**
- Cut nearing completion: target 183-185 lb
- If at target weight, shift to maintenance calories
- Keep protein at 200g+ regardless

**Checkpoints (end of Week 13):**
- ✅ BW: 183-185 lb
- ✅ Back squat still ≥ 355 lb (acceptable to lose 10 lb off peak during cut)
- ✅ Power clean singles feeling explosive at 80%+
- ✅ Approach touch: hand clearly over rim (not just fingertips)
- ✅ Dunk attempts: ball above rim level, control is the remaining gap

**If checkpoint fails:**
- Weight not coming down? Re-audit nutrition compliance — is the deficit real?
- Strength dropped too much? Pause cut, add a 2-week maintenance block, then resume.
- Jump metrics flat? Add 1 dedicated sprint session — sometimes the problem is approach speed, not jump power.

---

### Phase 4 — Realization (Weeks 14-16)
*"Peak for the dunk. Reduce fatigue, maximize expression."*

**Why this phase exists:** This is a taper. Training volume drops significantly so accumulated fatigue dissipates and the nervous system can express maximum power. This is where the dunk happens.

**Training focus:**
- Squat: 2x/week, low volume (2x2-3 at 80-85%), no grinding
- Olympic lifts: singles at 82-88%, 2x/week — peaking
- Jump training: 3-4x/week but LOW volume, HIGH quality. 8-12 max-effort approach jumps per session. Quality over quantity.
- Dunk attempts every jump session — this is now a skill practice, not conditioning
- Upper body: 1x/week or drop entirely
- Flexibility: maintain daily minimums, no new ROM work

**Nutrition focus:**
- At target weight (183-185): eat at maintenance
- If still slightly above: gentle 200-cal deficit, no aggressive cutting during peak

**Checkpoints (end of Week 16):**
- ✅ BW: 183-185 lb, stable
- ✅ Feeling fast, light, explosive — not fatigued
- ✅ Approach touch: 2+ inches above rim
- ✅ DUNK ACHIEVED or very close (ball above rim, palming it over)

**If checkpoint fails:**
- Still no dunk? Extend this phase 2 more weeks. Sometimes the taper needs more time.
- Feeling flat/fatigued? Extra rest day. Reduce everything except approach jump practice.

---

### Phase 5 — Consolidation + Next Arc (Weeks 17-18)
*"Lock it in. Test it. Plan what's next."*

**Why this phase exists:** Whether the dunk happened or not, this phase assesses where everything landed, tests all key metrics, and sets up the next arc (Olympic lifting competition prep, continued basketball, new strength targets).

**Training focus:**
- Testing week: back squat max, clean & jerk max, vertical jump max, approach touch max, side split measurement
- Light training to maintain
- Review all data from the arc

**Nutrition focus:**
- Maintenance calories
- Establish new body composition baseline

**Output of this phase:**
- Full metrics assessment (all lifts, jump, flexibility, body comp)
- Arc retrospective: what worked, what didn't, what to change
- Next arc plan initiated (likely: Olympic lifting competition prep as primary, dunk maintenance, new strength targets)

## Failure Mode Guardrails

These are structural rules, not notes. The plan enforces them.

### "I fell off for a week"
**Protocol:** Resume at Phase current, Week current. Do NOT restart. Use RPE 6 for the comeback session, then resume prescribed loads next session. Missing a week costs 1 week, not 4 weeks of motivation spiral.

### "Program is too complex"
**Guardrail:** Maximum 6-7 exercises per session. Fixed exercise menu per block. 1 focus per session (lower power, upper, lower strength, upper, jump). If a session takes >75 minutes, it's too long — cut the last accessory.

### "I'm not tracking"
**Guardrail:** The daily text includes PRs to beat. Logging is "did it" or set-by-set. If 3 consecutive sessions go unlogged, the system flags it — not with guilt, but with "what's making logging hard? Let's fix that."

### "Shoulder/wrist flares up"
**Guardrail:** Pain reported at 3+ → system proposes exercise swaps within 24 hours. Pain at 5+ → system proposes session modification (reduce load 20%, swap movements). Pain at 7+ → system recommends rest day and physio appointment. Never ignored, never auto-resolved.

### "Cut is stalling"
**Guardrail:** If 7-day BW average hasn't moved in 2 weeks, flag for nutritionist review. Options: increase deficit by 100 cal, add 1 conditioning session, or audit meal compliance. Never crash diet.

### "I'm second-guessing the plan"
**Guardrail:** Every exercise in every session links back to a phase goal, which links to the arc goal. The "why" is always one tap away. If the athlete can't see why they're doing something, the plan has failed — not the athlete.

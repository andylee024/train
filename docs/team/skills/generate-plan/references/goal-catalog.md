# Goal Catalog

Goals that coaches can propose. Each goal has a slot cost, a measurable metric, and reasoning for when to propose it. Coaches should tailor these to the athlete's profile — adjust current/target values based on the athlete's actual numbers.

Goals are organized by proposing coach. The Head Coach can propose from any category but typically sticks to meta-goals (consistency, habit).

---

## Strength Coach Goals

### Iron Ratio
- **Type:** Strength
- **Metric:** Back squat at bodyweight ratio
- **Example:** BS 370 → 405 lb while cutting to 185 lb (2.0× → 2.19× BW)
- **Cost:** 3 slots (2 squat sessions + 1 pull/press session per week)
- **When to propose:** Athlete has a strength base but needs to push it further, especially during a cut where maintaining or gaining strength matters. Good pairing with vertical goals since squat strength feeds jump power.

### Press Authority
- **Type:** Strength
- **Metric:** Overhead press or bench press target
- **Example:** OH Press 50 → 60 kg, or Bench 100 → 115 kg
- **Cost:** 2 slots (2 pressing sessions per week)
- **When to propose:** Athlete has an upper body weakness relative to lower body, or wants balanced strength. Modify for shoulder injuries — may shift to incline or floor press.

### Pull Power
- **Type:** Strength
- **Metric:** Weighted chin-up or deadlift target
- **Example:** Weighted chin-up 25 → 50 kg 5RM, or Deadlift 180 → 200 kg
- **Cost:** 2 slots (2 pull sessions per week)
- **When to propose:** Athlete's pull strength lags their push/squat. Good for overall athleticism and injury prevention.

## Sport Coach Goals

### Sky Reach
- **Type:** Vertical
- **Metric:** Approach touch height or standing vertical
- **Example:** Rim grab → 10" above rim, or standing vert 28" → 34"
- **Cost:** 3 slots (2 jump/plyo sessions + 1 sprint session per week)
- **When to propose:** The dunk goal or any vertical jump target. This is usually the north star for athletes chasing the dunk. Requires a strength base — pairs with Iron Ratio. If the athlete lacks strength, the Sport Coach may defer to building that first.

### Sprint Machine
- **Type:** Sport Performance
- **Metric:** Sprint time or acceleration metric
- **Example:** 40yd dash improvement, or 10m acceleration target
- **Cost:** 2 slots (2 sprint sessions per week)
- **When to propose:** Athlete wants speed for a sport (basketball, soccer) or speed supports their primary goal (approach velocity for dunking). Can share slots with jump training.

## Olympic Lifting Coach Goals

### Fast Hips
- **Type:** Olympic
- **Metric:** Clean & Jerk target
- **Example:** C&J 100 → 120 kg
- **Cost:** 3 slots (3 Olympic lifting sessions per week)
- **When to propose:** Athlete has Olympic lifting experience or access to coaching. High transfer to vertical jump (hip extension power, triple extension). Expensive in slots but high ROI for athletic development.

### Snatch Position
- **Type:** Olympic
- **Metric:** Snatch target or overhead squat mobility
- **Example:** Snatch 60 → 80 kg, or OHS at 40 kg for 3×3
- **Cost:** 2 slots (2 snatch-focused sessions per week)
- **When to propose:** Athlete wants full Olympic lifting development. Good for shoulder health and overhead position. Don't propose if athlete has active shoulder injury unless modified to power snatch/muscle snatch.

### Clean Foundation
- **Type:** Olympic
- **Metric:** Power clean or clean pull target
- **Example:** Power Clean 80 → 100 kg
- **Cost:** 2 slots (Olympic lift embedded in other sessions)
- **When to propose:** Entry-level Olympic lifting. Good when the athlete wants Olympic lifting exposure without dedicating full sessions to it. Can be embedded in strength sessions.

## Mobility Coach Goals

### Side Split
- **Type:** Flexibility
- **Metric:** Hip-to-floor distance in side split, or degrees
- **Example:** 140° → 170° (or hip-to-floor 30cm → 10cm)
- **Cost:** 1 slot (daily 15-min embedded in sessions, not a full training slot)
- **When to propose:** Athlete has flexibility goals or needs hip mobility for other movements (deep squat, high kicks, martial arts). Low cost, long timeline. Almost always worth including.

### Overhead Freedom
- **Type:** Mobility
- **Metric:** Overhead position quality
- **Example:** Full overhead squat with barbell, or OH snatch hold at 40 kg
- **Cost:** 1 slot (embedded mobility work)
- **When to propose:** Athlete needs better overhead position for Olympic lifts or pressing. Good complement to Snatch Position or Press Authority goals.

### Bulletproof
- **Type:** Mobility / Prehab
- **Metric:** Injury status improvement
- **Example:** Shoulder pain-free pressing, or wrist pain-free front rack
- **Cost:** 0 slots (embedded prehab in warm-ups)
- **When to propose:** Athlete has active injuries. This isn't an optional goal — if injuries exist, propose it. The modifications and prehab work happen within existing sessions.

## Nutritionist Goals

### Lean Machine
- **Type:** Body Comp
- **Metric:** Bodyweight and/or body fat target
- **Example:** 192 → 182-185 lb, 15% → 11% BF
- **Cost:** 0 slots (nutrition changes, not training time)
- **When to propose:** Athlete is above their target bodyweight or body fat. Supports power-to-weight ratio for jumping and athleticism. The Nutritionist sets calorie targets by block — usually a controlled deficit during build phases, maintenance during intensity phases.

### Fuel Right
- **Type:** Consistency
- **Metric:** Nutrition tracking adherence
- **Example:** Track calories/protein 6 of 7 days per week
- **Cost:** 0 slots
- **When to propose:** Athlete has identified nutrition as a weak point or has a history of falling off nutritional habits. Low cost, high impact when paired with body comp goals.

## Head Coach Goals

### Iron Habit
- **Type:** Consistency
- **Metric:** Training adherence
- **Example:** 5 sessions/week × 16 weeks (80 of 80)
- **Cost:** 0 slots (meta-goal, not session time)
- **When to propose:** Always propose this for athletes who have identified consistency as a challenge. It's the foundation — none of the other goals matter if the athlete doesn't show up. The Head Coach uses this to hold the line when life gets complicated.

### Arc Complete
- **Type:** Consistency
- **Metric:** Complete all blocks of the arc with exit tests passed
- **Example:** Pass 4 of 5 block exit tests
- **Cost:** 0 slots
- **When to propose:** Athlete is experienced and has had arcs fall apart in the past. This goal keeps the long game in focus.

## Tailoring Guidelines

When proposing goals to a specific athlete:

1. **Read the profile first.** Adjust current values and targets to match the athlete's actual numbers.
2. **Respect injuries.** Don't propose goals that conflict with active injuries without modification. The Mobility Coach should propose Bulletproof if any injuries exist.
3. **Match the north star.** If the athlete has a clear primary goal (e.g., "dunk"), lead with goals that serve it. Supporting goals are fine but the connection should be clear.
4. **Budget awareness.** Don't propose 15 slots worth of goals to a 10-slot athlete. Lead with the highest-priority proposals. It's better to propose 5-6 goals (some unchosen) than 10+ overwhelming options.
5. **0-slot goals are free.** Consistency and nutrition goals don't compete for training time. They're almost always worth proposing.
